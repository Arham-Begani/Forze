## Before You Write: Brand Voice Guidelines

### Forze Brand Voice
- **Tone**: Direct, no-BS, founder-to-founder honesty
- **Authority**: Data-driven, cite real numbers, reference Forze's actual outputs
- **Empathy**: Acknowledge founder pain points without being patronizing
- **Action-oriented**: Every section ends with a "next step" or insight
- **Specificity**: Concrete examples > generic advice
- **Examples format**: Use real founder stories from Forze's testimonials where possible

### Forze Value Proposition (Weave into each post)
- Skip 3-month hiring cycles → validate in 5 minutes
- Move with evidence, not guesses
- Research + Brand + Landing Page + Feasibility in one system
- Founder-friendly (no code required)

### Do's:
✅ Use "you" (founder-to-founder)  
✅ Include real data (TAM, costs, timelines)  
✅ Reference Forze outputs naturally (not salesy)  
✅ Internal link aggressively (2-4 per post)  
✅ Use short sentences + white space (scannable)  
✅ Include FAQ sections (ranks for People Also Ask)  
✅ Add real-world failure stories (builds credibility)  

### Don'ts:
❌ Generic startup advice (already saturated)  
❌ Over-explain basic concepts  
❌ Use "I think" or "probably" (be certain or cite source)  
❌ Salesy language ("Only $99/month!")  
❌ Too many bullet points (mix formats)  
❌ Sales CTAs in middle sections (only at end)

#### SECTION 4: The Validation Process Step-by-Step (400 words)

**Step 1: Define Your Hypothesis (10 minutes)**
- Write down: "We believe X customer has Y problem, worth Z to solve"
- Example: "Insurance agents waste 4 hours/day on scheduling, would pay $100/mo"
- Why it matters: Validation tests this hypothesis, not your personal belief

**Step 2: Research the Market (30 minutes)**
- Google Trends for search volume
- Competitor review (what are they charging?)
- Community research (Reddit, Indie Hackers, LinkedIn groups)
- Real data: How many potential customers exist? (TAM estimation)

**Step 3: Talk to Customers (2-4 hours)**
- 10-20 real interviews with people in target customer segment
- Ask: "Do you have this problem? Would you pay $X for solution?"
- Don't pitch. Listen. Take notes.
- Red flag: If 70%+ say "no" or "maybe," hypothesis is weak

**Step 4: Build (or Mock) Minimal Test**
- No code needed: Landing page + email capture
- Mock it: Figma prototype or Carrd page
- Test it: Drive traffic (Twitter, communities, ads)
- Measure: "Did people sign up? Did they respond to problem description?"

**Step 5: Interpret Results**
- 50+ signups on landing page testing = strong signal
- 5-10 signups = weak signal
- 0 signups = either wrong market or wrong targeting

- Problem: Paralysis by analysis (more research, no shipping)
